About Me

I hail from Barrackpore in West Bengal, which has great historical significance.

I was born on 15th April, which is celebrated as Pahela Baisakh in West Bengal — the Bengali New Year — and marked with great pomp and show in many Indian states.

After completing my schooling in Barrackpore, I pursued my Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from St. Xavier's College, Kolkata, followed by a Master of Science in Mathematics from the University of Calcutta.

In 2015, I was awarded the Research Award in Mathematics by the National Board for Higher Mathematics (NBHM). I also secured the Junior Research Fellowship from the Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR).

I completed my Ph.D. in Algebraic Graph Theory from the Department of Pure Mathematics, University of Calcutta in 2022. During my doctoral research, I published 16 research papers, of which 12 are single-authored.

Currently, I am working as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics, JIS University. Recently, I received the prestigious JIS Samman Award for the highest number of publications in an academic year.

My research interests include Combinatorics, Matrix Theory, and Algebraic Graph Theory.
